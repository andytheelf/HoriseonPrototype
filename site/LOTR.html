    J.R.R Tolkien

    If more of us valued food and cheer and song above hoarded gold, it would be a merrier world.

    Born January 3rd 1892 in Bloemfontein (now Free State Province) South Africa to mother Mabel Tolkien and Arthur Reuel Tolkien. At the age of 3 his mother took him and his younger brother, Hilary Arthur Reuel Tolkien, to England to visit family. However while in England his father died of rheumatic fever, turning what was supposed to be an extended visit into a permanant move. Tolkien met his future wife Edith when he was 16. Coming from similar backgrounds and being of similar dispositions they quickly fell in love. However, Tolkien's guardian did not approve of the two, and forbade Tolkien from seeing her until he turned 21. Tolkien obeyed, and on the eve of his 21st birthday wrote to Edith asking her to marry him. She accepted his proposal, and broke off her other engagement.

    In 1914 Britain entered WWII. Tolkien endured much criticism from his family for finishing school before enlisting. But from 1915 to 1920 he fought in France. Reurning to England after the war Tolkien took up a teaching position at The University of Leeds. That was the beginning of career as a professor, and his journey towards creating foundation of fantasy literature as we know it today.
